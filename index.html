Profile

Researcher, educator,
system builder.

I am a PhD scholar at University College Dublin, researching the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), AI, and healthcare. My work focuses on making well-being explainable and teachable, ensuring AI systems support human flourishing in high-stakes contexts.

With a background spanning language education and digital health, I translate conceptual frameworks into implementable AI-supported systems. I have contributed to multi-national projects on Long-COVID and cardiovascular risk management with the Irish Heart Foundation and European partners.

Research Programme

What I'm building.

"My work focuses on addressing the gap between human decision-making capacity and AI-mediated environments by designing systems that preserve agency, clarity, and effective action."

System Overview

Input

Behavioural, contextual, and longitudinal well-being data from social microcosms.

Process

Modelling well-being as a functional capacity via the Spectrum of Contextual Well-being (SCW).

Output

Adaptive feedback mechanisms and agency-preserving intervention strategies.

Spectrum of Contextual Well-being

A functional framework mapping perception and judgment across complex AI environments.

ContextWell Lab

An early-stage research lab and intervention system born from my PhD. It serves as an incubator to translate theoretical well-being frameworks into scalable AI applications and concrete clinical outcomes.

AI Governance & Agency

Reframing human oversight as a dimension of well-being efficacy rather than just compliance.
